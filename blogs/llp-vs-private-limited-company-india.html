LLP vs Private Limited Company — Which is Better for Your Business in India?

✍️ CA Kunal Chhabra, FCA 📅 March 12, 2025 ⏱ 7 min read 🌐 Pan India Online Service
💬 WhatsApp Us 📞 Call Now

Overview

Two of the most popular business structures in India are the Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) and the Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd). Both offer limited liability protection but differ significantly in compliance, taxation, and funding options.

Quick Comparison

ParameterLLPPvt Ltd
Minimum Members2 Partners2 Directors
Registration Cost₹5,000–8,000₹8,000–15,000
Annual ComplianceMinimalHigher
Tax Rate30% flat22–25%
Equity Funding❌ Not possible✅ Yes
Statutory AuditOnly if turnover >₹40LMandatory
ESOP for Employees❌ No✅ Yes

Choose LLP If:

  • You run a professional services firm — CA, law, consulting, architecture
  • You want lower compliance burden and annual costs
  • You do not plan to raise equity funding from investors
  • Partners want flexible profit sharing without strict corporate rules
  • Expected turnover under ₹40 lakh initially

Choose Private Limited If:

  • You plan to raise funds from VCs, angels, or institutional investors
  • You want to offer ESOPs to attract and retain employees
  • You are building a scalable product or tech startup
  • You want the lower corporate tax rate of 22%
  • Your business requires high credibility with large clients

Annual Compliance Cost Comparison

Cost HeadLLPPvt Ltd
Annual ROC Filings₹3,000–5,000₹5,000–10,000
Statutory AuditNot always required₹10,000–25,000
ITR Filing₹3,000–6,000₹5,000–10,000

Verdict

For most startups and product companies — go with Private Limited Company. For professional services, consulting, and small trading firms — LLP is more practical and cost-effective. When in doubt, consult a CA — the right structure saves significant money over years.
