Startup India & DPIIT Registration — Complete Guide 2025

What is DPIIT Recognition?

The Startup India initiative provides eligible startups with DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) recognition — unlocking significant tax benefits, funding opportunities, and compliance relaxations under the Startup India Action Plan.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Incorporated as Private Limited Company, LLP, or Partnership Firm
  • Not older than 10 years from date of incorporation
  • Annual turnover should not exceed ₹100 crore in any financial year
  • Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/processes
  • Not formed by splitting or reconstructing an existing business

Key Benefits of DPIIT Recognition

  • 🎯 Tax Holiday: 3 years income tax exemption out of first 10 years (Section 80-IAC)
  • 💰 Angel Tax Exemption: No tax on funding received from investors (Section 56(2)(viib))
  • ⚡ Fast Track Exit: Wind up company in 90 days instead of years
  • 🏛️ Government Tenders: Exempt from prior experience / turnover criteria
  • 🔬 IPR Benefits: 80% rebate on patent filing fees, fast-track examination
  • 📋 Self-certification: Under 9 labour and environment laws
  • 💼 Funding Access: SIDBI Fund of Funds, Startup India Seed Fund Scheme

Step-by-Step Registration Process

  1. Incorporate your entity first — must be Pvt Ltd, LLP, or Partnership
  2. Go to startupindia.gov.in → Create account
  3. Fill application — company details, incorporation date, sector, brief description of innovation
  4. Upload — COI, MOA/AOA or LLP Agreement, brief on innovation/unique offering
  5. Submit → DPIIT reviews and grants recognition (usually 2–7 working days)
  6. Download your DPIIT Recognition Certificate

CA Certificate for Section 80-IAC Tax Holiday

The DPIIT recognition itself is free, but to claim the 3-year income tax holiday under Section 80-IAC, you need to apply separately to the Inter-Ministerial Board with a certificate from a Practicing Chartered Accountant confirming that your startup meets all eligibility conditions.

Documents Required

  • Certificate of Incorporation (COI)
  • MOA & AOA / LLP Agreement
  • PAN Card of entity
  • Brief description of innovation (200–500 words)
  • Pitch deck (optional but recommended)
