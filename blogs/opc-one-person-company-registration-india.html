One Person Company (OPC) Registration in India — Complete Guide 2025

What is a One Person Company (OPC)?

A One Person Company (OPC) is a unique business structure introduced under the Companies Act, 2013 that allows a single individual to incorporate and run a company with all the benefits of a Private Limited Company — limited liability, separate legal entity, and professional credibility — without needing a second person as co-founder or partner.

OPC bridges the gap between a sole proprietorship and a Private Limited Company, making it the ideal structure for individual entrepreneurs, freelancers, consultants, and solo business owners who want the protection and credibility of a company.

Key Benefits of OPC

  • Single Person Control: Sole director and shareholder — complete control over all decisions
  • Limited Liability: Personal assets fully protected — liability limited to share capital
  • Separate Legal Entity: OPC can own property, enter contracts, open bank accounts in its own name
  • Professional Credibility: Clients and banks trust a registered company far more than a proprietorship
  • Easier Bank Loans: Banks prefer lending to registered companies over proprietorships
  • Reduced Compliance: Less compliance burden compared to Private Limited Company
  • Perpetual Succession: Business continues via the nominee even in case of death of the sole member

Eligibility for OPC Registration

  • Only a natural person who is an Indian citizen and resident can form an OPC
  • Resident means stayed in India for at least 182 days in the previous calendar year
  • One person can be a member of only one OPC at a time
  • A minor cannot be a member or nominee of an OPC
  • Cannot be converted into a Section 8 (non-profit) company
  • NRIs are now also eligible to form OPC (amended rules)

Mandatory Nominee — Key Requirement

Every OPC must have a nominee — an Indian citizen who will become the sole member of the OPC in case of death or incapacity of the current sole member. The nominee must:

  • Give written consent in Form INC-3
  • Be an Indian citizen and resident
  • Not be a member of another OPC
  • Can be changed at any time by the sole member

Documents Required

For the Sole Director/Shareholder:

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card / Passport / Voter ID
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Bank statement or utility bill (not older than 2 months)

For the Nominee:

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Photograph
  • Written consent in Form INC-3

For Registered Office:

  • Electricity bill / property tax receipt
  • NOC from property owner
  • Rent agreement (if applicable)

OPC Registration Process

  1. Obtain DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) for the sole director
  2. Reserve company name via RUN on MCA portal
  3. Draft MOA and AOA
  4. Obtain nominee's consent in Form INC-3
  5. File SPICe+ form with all documents
  6. Receive Certificate of Incorporation with CIN ✅

Mandatory Conversion Rules

An OPC must mandatorily convert to a Private Limited Company if:

  • Paid-up share capital exceeds ₹50 lakh, OR
  • Annual turnover exceeds ₹2 crore

An OPC can also voluntarily convert to a Private Limited Company at any time after 2 years of incorporation.

Annual Compliance for OPC

  • Statutory Audit — mandatory every year
  • AOC-4 — Financial Statements filing within 180 days of financial year end
  • MGT-7A — Annual Return within 60 days of financial year end
  • Income Tax Return — ITR-6 for companies
  • DIR-3 KYC — Director KYC annually

Who Should Register an OPC?

  • Freelancers and independent consultants wanting corporate credibility
  • Solo entrepreneurs who don't have a co-founder
  • Professionals wanting to upgrade from proprietorship
  • Coaches, trainers, designers, developers working independently
  • Small business owners wanting limited liability protection
