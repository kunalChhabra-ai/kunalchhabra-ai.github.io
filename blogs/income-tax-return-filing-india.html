Income Tax Return (ITR) Filing in India — Complete Guide for FY 2024-25

What is Income Tax Return (ITR)?

An Income Tax Return (ITR) is a prescribed form through which a taxpayer — individual, HUF, firm, company, or other entity — declares their income earned during a financial year, deductions claimed, taxes paid, and tax liability to the Income Tax Department of India. Filing an ITR is a legal obligation for eligible taxpayers and also serves as an important financial document for loan applications, visa processing, and business credibility.

Who Must File an ITR?

  • Individuals with gross total income exceeding ₹2.5 lakh (₹3 lakh for senior citizens, ₹5 lakh for super senior citizens)
  • Companies and firms — mandatory regardless of profit or loss
  • Individuals claiming tax refund on TDS deducted
  • Individuals with assets or financial interest outside India
  • Individuals with foreign income or foreign assets
  • Individuals who have deposited more than ₹1 crore in bank accounts during the year
  • Individuals who have spent more than ₹2 lakh on foreign travel
  • Individuals with electricity consumption exceeding ₹1 lakh
  • Professionals with gross receipts exceeding ₹10 lakh
  • Any person whose ITR filing is mandatory as per tax authority notices

Key Deadlines for FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26)

  • 31st July 2025 — Individuals, HUFs, firms (not requiring audit)
  • 31st October 2025 — Taxpayers requiring audit under Income Tax Act or other laws
  • 30th November 2025 — Transfer pricing cases
  • 31st December 2025 — Belated return (with late fee under Section 234F)
  • Note: Missing the deadline does not mean you cannot file — belated and updated returns are available but attract penalties and interest

New Tax Regime vs Old Tax Regime — FY 2024-25

From FY 2023-24, the New Tax Regime is the default regime. To use the Old Regime and claim deductions, you must specifically opt for it before filing your return.

Standard Deduction: ₹75,000 under New Regime | ₹50,000 under Old Regime

New Regime is beneficial for taxpayers with fewer deductions. Old Regime is better if you have significant 80C investments, HRA, home loan interest, etc. A CA can calculate which regime saves more tax for your specific situation.

Documents Required for ITR Filing

  • Form 16 — issued by employer (for salaried employees)
  • Form 16A — TDS certificate for non-salary income (FDs, professional fees)
  • Form 26AS and AIS (Annual Information Statement) — download from income tax portal
  • Bank statements for all accounts during the year
  • Investment proofs — 80C (PPF, ELSS, LIC), 80D (health insurance), HRA receipts
  • Home loan interest certificate from bank
  • Capital gains statement from broker (for stocks, mutual funds)
  • Rent receipts if claiming HRA exemption
  • Business income details — P&L, balance sheet (for business owners)

Consequences of Not Filing ITR

  • Late fee under Section 234F: ₹5,000 (₹1,000 if income below ₹5 lakh)
  • Interest under Section 234A: 1% per month on unpaid tax
  • Loss cannot be carried forward: Business and capital losses lapse if return not filed on time
  • Prosecution: In serious cases, non-filing can lead to prosecution under Section 276CC
  • Scrutiny risk: Non-filers are frequently picked up for scrutiny by the department

Benefits of Filing ITR Even if Not Mandatory

  • Essential document for home loans, car loans, personal loans
  • Required for visa applications to USA, UK, Canada, Schengen countries
  • Proof of income for insurance policies
  • Establishes financial track record for business credibility
  • Required to claim tax refund on TDS deducted
  • Carry forward losses to offset future income
