GST Registration for New Business in India — Who Needs It & How to Apply 2025

✍️ CA Kunal Chhabra, FCA  |  📅 March 2025  |  ⏱ 7 min read  |  🌐 Pan India Online

What is GST Registration?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) Registration is the process by which a business obtains a unique GSTIN (GST Identification Number) — a 15-digit PAN-based number that authorizes the business to collect GST from customers, claim Input Tax Credit on purchases, and file GST returns with the government.

GST registration is not just a legal requirement for eligible businesses — it is also a mark of credibility that opens doors to larger clients, government contracts, and interstate business operations.

Who Needs GST Registration? — Mandatory Cases

  • Goods suppliers with annual turnover exceeding ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for special category states)
  • Service providers with annual turnover exceeding ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for special category states)
  • Interstate suppliers — any business making inter-state supplies of goods or services, regardless of turnover
  • E-commerce sellers — selling on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or any online marketplace, regardless of turnover
  • E-commerce operators — platforms facilitating supply of goods/services
  • Casual taxable persons — businesses operating temporarily in a state where they have no fixed establishment
  • Non-resident taxable persons — foreign companies supplying in India
  • Persons liable under Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)
  • Input Service Distributors (ISD)
  • Businesses required to deduct TDS under GST

Special Category States — Lower Threshold

States where GST registration threshold is lower (₹10 lakh for services, ₹20 lakh for goods):

  • Manipur, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura — ₹10 lakh threshold
  • Meghalaya, Uttarakhand, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh — ₹10 lakh threshold
  • Himachal Pradesh, Puducherry — ₹10 lakh threshold

Voluntary GST Registration — Why Register Even if Not Mandatory?

  • Input Tax Credit (ITC): Claim GST paid on all business purchases — significantly reduces effective tax burden
  • B2B Client Requirement: Most corporate clients and large businesses only deal with GST-registered vendors
  • Government Tenders: GST registration is mandatory for most government and PSU tenders
  • Interstate Business: Without GST registration, interstate supplies are not permissible
  • Business Credibility: GSTIN on invoices signals professionalism and compliance
  • Startup Funding: Investors prefer GST-registered companies during due diligence

Documents Required for GST Registration

For Proprietorship:

  • PAN Card of proprietor
  • Aadhaar Card of proprietor
  • Photograph of proprietor
  • Bank account details — cancelled cheque or bank statement
  • Business address proof — electricity bill not older than 2 months
  • NOC from owner (if rented premises)

For Private Limited Company / LLP:

  • PAN Card of company/LLP
  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • MOA and AOA / LLP Agreement
  • PAN and Aadhaar of all directors/partners
  • Photographs of all directors/partners
  • Board resolution authorizing GST application
  • Bank account details of company
  • Business address proof
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of authorized signatory

Step-by-Step GST Registration Process

  1. Go to GST Portal: Visit gst.gov.in → Services → Registration → New Registration
  2. Part A — Basic Details: Enter PAN, email, mobile number → OTP verification → Temporary Reference Number (TRN) generated
  3. Part B — Complete Application: Log in with TRN → fill in complete business details — business name, nature of business, HSN/SAC codes, business address
  4. Upload Documents: All required documents in specified format and size
  5. Submit Application: Sign with DSC (for companies) or Aadhaar OTP e-sign (for individuals)
  6. Application Reference Number (ARN): Generated immediately — track application status
  7. GST Officer Review: Officer may raise queries via Form REG-03 — must respond within 7 working days
  8. GSTIN Issued: Within 3-7 working days for complete, error-free applications

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Entering incorrect HSN/SAC codes for your products or services
  • Using old address proof documents older than 2 months
  • Mismatch between PAN name and business name
  • Not adding all business verticals or additional places of business
  • Missing the Composition Scheme option for eligible small businesses
  • Not filing GST returns on time after registration — late fees start from day one
  • Claiming incorrect Input Tax Credit — can attract penalties and interest

GST Composition Scheme — For Small Businesses

Small businesses with turnover below ₹1.5 crore (₹75 lakh for some states / service providers) can opt for the Composition Scheme — pay a flat percentage of turnover as GST (1%-6%) instead of regular GST rates. However, they cannot claim ITC or make interstate supplies. Consult a CA to decide if Composition Scheme is beneficial for your business.
