Private Limited Company Registration in India — Complete Guide 2025

What is a Private Limited Company?

A Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd) is the most popular and credible business structure in India, governed by the Companies Act, 2013 and regulated by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). It offers limited liability protection, a separate legal identity, and the ability to raise equity funding — making it the preferred choice for startups, SMEs, and growing businesses across India.

Key Features of a Private Limited Company

  • Separate Legal Entity: The company exists independently of its directors and shareholders. It can own assets, enter into contracts, and sue or be sued in its own name.
  • Limited Liability: Shareholders are only liable to the extent of their share capital. Personal assets are fully protected.
  • Perpetual Succession: The company continues to exist even if directors or shareholders change.
  • Easy Funding: Can issue shares to investors, VCs, and angel investors. Most preferred structure for startup funding.
  • ESOP Facility: Can issue Employee Stock Options to attract and retain top talent.
  • High Credibility: Clients, vendors, and banks trust Private Limited Companies more than proprietorships or partnerships.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum 2 Directors — maximum 15
  • Minimum 2 Shareholders — maximum 200
  • At least one director must be an Indian Resident (stayed in India for at least 182 days in the previous calendar year)
  • A valid registered office address in India
  • No minimum paid-up capital requirement — minimum authorized capital is ₹1 lakh
  • Directors must not be disqualified under the Companies Act, 2013

Documents Required

For Each Director and Shareholder:

  • PAN Card — mandatory for Indian nationals
  • Aadhaar Card / Passport / Voter ID / Driving License — identity proof
  • Recent passport-size photograph
  • Latest bank statement or utility bill — not older than 2 months (address proof)
  • Email ID and mobile number linked to Aadhaar (preferred)
  • For foreign nationals — passport mandatory, apostilled/notarized documents required

For Registered Office Address:

  • Latest electricity bill or property tax receipt of the premises
  • NOC (No Objection Certificate) from the property owner
  • Rent agreement if the premises are rented

Step-by-Step Registration Process

  1. Digital Signature Certificate (DSC): Obtained for all proposed directors. Required to sign MCA e-forms digitally. Takes 1-2 working days.
  2. Director Identification Number (DIN): A unique identification number for each director. Applied along with incorporation via SPICe+ form — no separate application needed.
  3. Name Reservation via RUN: Reserve your company name through the MCA portal. Submit up to 2 name options. Approved names are reserved for 20 days.
  4. Draft MOA and AOA: Memorandum of Association (objects and powers of the company) and Articles of Association (internal rules and regulations) are drafted.
  5. File SPICe+ Form: The Single Point of Contact for Incorporation form integrates — company incorporation, PAN, TAN, GST registration, EPFO, ESIC, and bank account opening — all in one form.
  6. Certificate of Incorporation (COI): Issued by the Registrar of Companies with a unique Corporate Identity Number (CIN). Your company is officially registered.

Timeline for Registration

  • DSC: 1-2 working days
  • Name approval via RUN: 2-3 working days
  • SPICe+ filing and MCA processing: 3-7 working days
  • Total: 7-15 working days from document submission (error-free applications)

Post-Incorporation Compliance

Registration is just the beginning. After receiving your Certificate of Incorporation, the following steps are mandatory:

  • INC-20A: Declaration of Commencement of Business — must be filed within 180 days of incorporation. Non-filing attracts heavy penalties.
  • Open Current Bank Account: In the company's name — never mix personal and company finances.
  • Appoint Statutory Auditor: Within 30 days of incorporation — file Form ADT-1.
  • GST Registration: If turnover is expected to exceed the threshold or if you're making inter-state supplies.
  • First Board Meeting: Must be held within 30 days of incorporation.
  • Annual ROC Filings: MGT-7 (Annual Return) and AOC-4 (Financial Statements) every year.
  • Statutory Audit: Mandatory every financial year regardless of turnover.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Choosing a name too similar to an existing company or registered trademark
  • Document mismatch — names, dates, addresses must match exactly across all documents
  • Not filing INC-20A within 180 days — directors can be disqualified
  • Mixing personal and company bank accounts
  • Missing annual ROC filing deadlines — ₹100 per day penalty per form
  • Not maintaining statutory registers and minutes of meetings
