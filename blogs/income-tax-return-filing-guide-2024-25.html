ITR Form Selection Guide FY 2024-25

ITR FormWho Should File
ITR-1 (Sahaj)Salaried individuals, income up to ₹50 lakh, one house property
ITR-2Capital gains, multiple properties, foreign income, director in company
ITR-3Business or profession income, partner in firm
ITR-4 (Sugam)Presumptive business income under 44AD / 44ADA / 44AE
ITR-5Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs
ITR-6Companies other than Section 11 exemption companies
ITR-7Trusts, political parties, charitable institutions, Section 8 companies

Key Deadlines FY 2024-25

  • Non-audit cases (individuals/salaried): 31st July 2025
  • Audit cases (businesses with audit requirement): 31st October 2025
  • Transfer pricing cases: 30th November 2025
  • Belated return: 31st December 2025 (with late fee)

Documents Needed for ITR Filing

  • Form 16 (from employer for salaried individuals)
  • Bank statements for all accounts
  • Investment proofs — 80C, 80D, HRA, LTA receipts
  • Capital gains statements from broker
  • Home loan interest certificate
  • Rental income details if any
  • AIS (Annual Information Statement) from Income Tax portal

New Tax Regime vs Old Tax Regime

From FY 2024-25, the New Tax Regime is the default. You must specifically opt for the Old Regime to claim deductions like 80C, 80D, HRA etc.

AspectNew RegimeOld Regime
Default✅ YesMust opt-in
80C Deductions❌ Not available✅ Up to ₹1.5L
HRA Exemption❌ Not available✅ Available
Standard Deduction₹75,000₹50,000
Best forLow investmentsHigh deductions

A CA can calculate which regime saves more tax for your specific income and investment profile.
