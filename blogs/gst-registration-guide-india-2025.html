GST Registration — Complete Guide for New Businesses in India 2025

✍️ CA Kunal Chhabra, FCA 📅 March 14, 2025 ⏱ 6 min read 🌐 Pan India Online Service
💬 WhatsApp Us 📞 Call Now

When is GST Registration Mandatory?

  • Turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh/year — for goods suppliers (₹20 lakh for special category states)
  • Turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh/year — for service providers (₹10 lakh for special category states)
  • Inter-state supply — selling across state borders regardless of turnover
  • E-commerce sellers — selling on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho etc. regardless of turnover
  • Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) — receiving supplies under RCM
  • Casual taxable persons — temporary business in another state

Benefits of Voluntary GST Registration

  • ✅ Claim Input Tax Credit on all business purchases — reduces costs
  • ✅ Corporate clients prefer GST-registered vendors
  • ✅ Required for most government tenders
  • ✅ GSTIN on invoices builds credibility and trust
  • ✅ Enables interstate business operations smoothly

Documents Required for GST Registration

  • PAN Card of business / company
  • Aadhaar Card of proprietor / directors
  • Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)
  • Address proof of business premises (utility bill not older than 2 months)
  • Certificate of Incorporation for companies / LLPs
  • MOA & AOA for companies
  • Photograph and digital signature of authorized person
  • Board resolution authorizing GST application (for companies)

Step-by-Step GST Registration Process

  1. Go to gst.gov.in → New Registration → Form REG-01
  2. Enter PAN, email, mobile → OTP verification
  3. Fill in business details, business nature, HSN/SAC codes
  4. Upload all required documents
  5. Submit with DSC or e-sign (Aadhaar OTP)
  6. GST officer reviews — may raise queries via REG-03
  7. GSTIN issued within 3–7 working days for complete applications

Common GST Registration Mistakes to Avoid

  • Entering wrong HSN/SAC codes for your products/services
  • Using old or mismatched address proofs
  • Not adding all business verticals or branches
  • Missing the composition scheme option for eligible small businesses
  • Delay in filing returns after registration — attracts late fees from day 1
