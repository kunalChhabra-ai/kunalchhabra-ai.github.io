How to Register a Private Limited Company in India (2025 Guide)

What is a Private Limited Company?

A Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd) is a legally recognized business entity under the Companies Act, 2013. It offers limited liability protection, separate legal identity, and easier access to funding — making it the most preferred structure for startups and growing businesses in India.

Why Choose Private Limited Company?

  • Limited Liability: Personal assets of directors/shareholders are protected
  • Separate Legal Entity: Company can own property, enter contracts, sue and be sued
  • Easy Funding: VCs, angel investors, and banks prefer Pvt Ltd structure
  • Credibility: Clients and vendors trust Pvt Ltd companies more
  • Tax Benefits: Corporate tax rate of 22-25% vs higher individual rates
  • Perpetual Succession: Company continues even if directors change

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum 2 Directors (maximum 15)
  • Minimum 2 Shareholders (maximum 200)
  • At least one director must be an Indian Resident
  • Minimum authorized capital: ₹1 lakh
  • A registered office address in India

Documents Required

  • PAN Card of all directors (mandatory)
  • Aadhaar Card / Passport / Voter ID
  • Latest passport-size photograph
  • Bank statement / utility bill (not older than 2 months)
  • Electricity bill + NOC for registered office
  • Rent agreement (if rented premises)

Step-by-Step Registration Process

  1. Obtain DSC — Digital Signature Certificate for all directors (1-2 days)
  2. Apply for DIN — Director Identification Number via SPICe+ form
  3. Name Reservation via RUN — Submit 2 name options on MCA portal (1-3 days)
  4. Draft MOA & AOA — Memorandum and Articles of Association
  5. File SPICe+ — Single form for incorporation, PAN, TAN, GST (3-7 days)
  6. Receive COI — Certificate of Incorporation with CIN number

Timeline

  • DSC: 1–2 days
  • Name approval: 2–3 days
  • Incorporation: 3–7 working days
  • Total: 7–15 working days

Post-Incorporation Steps

  • Open current bank account in company name
  • File INC-20A within 180 days (mandatory)
  • Apply for GST Registration if applicable
  • Maintain statutory registers
  • Annual ROC filings — MGT-7 and AOC-4
