What is a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)?

A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is a hybrid business structure that combines the flexibility and tax benefits of a partnership with the limited liability protection of a company. Governed by the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008 and regulated by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), an LLP is a separate legal entity distinct from its partners.

LLP is particularly popular among professionals — CA firms, law firms, consulting agencies, architects, and technology service providers — where partners want operational flexibility with personal asset protection.

Key Features of LLP

  • Separate Legal Entity: LLP exists independently of its partners — can own assets, sue and be sued
  • Limited Liability: Each partner's liability is limited to their agreed contribution — personal assets protected
  • Flexible Management: Partners can decide management structure through the LLP Agreement
  • No Mandatory Audit: Statutory audit required only if turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh or capital exceeds ₹25 lakh
  • Lower Compliance: Fewer mandatory annual filings compared to Private Limited Company
  • No Dividend Distribution Tax: Profit distributed to partners is not subject to DDT
  • Perpetual Succession: LLP continues even if partners change

Who Should Choose LLP?

  • Professional service firms — CA firms, law firms, architect offices
  • Consulting and advisory businesses
  • IT and technology service companies
  • Businesses that do NOT plan to raise equity funding
  • Partners wanting flexible profit sharing arrangements
  • Businesses wanting lower annual compliance cost

Eligibility for LLP Registration

  • Minimum 2 Designated Partners — no maximum limit on total partners
  • At least one Designated Partner must be an Indian Resident
  • Any individual or body corporate can be a partner
  • Partners must not be of unsound mind or declared insolvent
  • Foreign nationals can be partners in an LLP (with RBI approval for certain sectors)

Documents Required

For Each Designated Partner:

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card / Passport / Voter ID / Driving License
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Bank statement or utility bill (not older than 2 months)
  • Email ID and mobile number
  • DPIN (Designated Partner Identification Number) — applied during registration

For Registered Office:

  • Electricity bill / property tax receipt
  • NOC from property owner
  • Rent agreement (if rented premises)

LLP Registration Process

  1. Obtain DSC: Digital Signature Certificate for all Designated Partners — 1-2 days
  2. Apply for DPIN: Designated Partner Identification Number — via FiLLiP form
  3. Reserve LLP Name: Via RUN-LLP on MCA portal — submit up to 2 name options
  4. Draft LLP Agreement: Key document defining rights, duties, profit sharing, and management of the LLP
  5. File FiLLiP Form: Form for incorporation of LLP — filed on MCA21 portal
  6. File LLP Agreement — Form 3: Must be filed within 30 days of incorporation
  7. Receive Certificate of Incorporation: With LLP Identification Number (LLPIN) ✅

LLP Agreement — Why It Matters

The LLP Agreement is the most critical document for an LLP. It defines:

  • Profit and loss sharing ratio among partners
  • Rights and duties of each partner
  • Decision-making process — voting rights
  • Procedure for admission of new partners
  • Procedure for retirement and exit of partners
  • Dispute resolution mechanism

A poorly drafted LLP Agreement can lead to serious disputes between partners. Always get it drafted by a qualified CA or lawyer.

Annual Compliance for LLP

  • Form 11 (Annual Return): Must be filed within 60 days of financial year end — due 30th May
  • Form 8 (Statement of Account and Solvency): Filed within 30 days of 6 months of financial year end — due 30th October
  • Income Tax Return (ITR-5): Filed annually
  • Tax Audit (if applicable): If turnover exceeds ₹1 crore
  • GST Returns: If registered under GST

Penalty for Non-Compliance

Late filing of Form 11 or Form 8 attracts a penalty of ₹100 per day per form with no upper limit. Partners can also face personal liability for non-compliance. Always file on time or engage a CA to manage compliance.
